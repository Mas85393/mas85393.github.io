Living a Healthy Life

The 3 Basic Steps to Living a Healthy Life

  • I. Eating Healthy
  • II. Excerise
  • III. Getting Lots of Rest

Being healhty involves more than exercising every once and a while and switching out that double cheeseburger for a salad, it involves effort and committment to your health. In order to live a long and healthy life you need to make healthy choices within your daily life that will help improve your lifestyle in the long run and short run. You can do this by following the 3 Basic Steps to Living a Healthy Life to make improvements that will benefit your health. By taking charge of your bad past habits and choosing to follow these 3 easy steps you will be feeling better about yourself in absolutely no time. The 3 Basic Steps to Living a Healthy Lifestyle are: Eating Healthy, Exercise, and Getting Lots of Rest. Throughout this article we will explore each of these steps further and help you to have a better understanding of what "Living a Healthy Life" actually entails.

I. Eathing Healthy

Its easier than you think to start eating healthy, a few simple changes to your daily diet will have you feeling healthier than ever! In todays world it is so easy for us to slip up, especially when it comes to our diet. Avoiding those tempting sweets and tasty fast food meals is definitly easier said than done but with enough self discipline and committment to your body and your health you will conquor those tasty temptations in no time. By adding these basic changes to your daily diet you will be cutting your calorie intake in half and giving your body more of what it wants and needs to function. Below are 6 simple steps that will help you change your current diet.

A. Eat Lots of Fruits and Vegtables

Make sure that you are consuming a lot of fruits and vegtables at each meal. At least half of your plate should be fruits and vegtables. TIP: The more colorful the food on your plate is the healthier your meal is!

B. Switch to Whole Grains

You should switch from consuming refined-grain foods to whole grains. For example you could switch from eating white bread to wheat bread, which is a much healthier choice.

C. Switch from Fat-Free Milk to Low-Fat Milk

Although both of these choices of milk have the same amount of calcium and other healhty nutrients the fat free choice is lower in calories which makes it the healthier choice.

D. Eat More Protien

Meat, seafood, dry beans, eggs, and nuts are all good sources of protien to add to your daily diet. Adding a healthy variety of protien in your diet will keep you feeling healthy and having more energy.

E. Drink Lots of Water

By making the choice to drink more water instead of sugary drinks you are cutting your calorie intake and hydrating your body at the same time. A healthy tip is trying to add slices of lemon, lime, or watermelon to your water if you want a splash of flavor.

F. Cut Back on the Sweets

This is way easier said than done but cutting back on those sugary sweets will help you limit your calorie intake and keep your body on the right track. Every once in a while its okay to treat yourself though!

II. Exercise

Exercise is a great way to get your body moving to help you achieve a more healthy and active lifestyle. Adding a mandatory workout into your daily schedule will help you to keep yourself accountable for working out everyday and have a plan in place as to what you need to do everyday of the week. By adding a healhty mixture of cardio and muscle training circuits will help you to keep your body moving and muslces strenghtening. III. Getting Lots of Rest

This may be the most simple answer to helping you live a healthy life but rest is key. Along with eating healthy and exercising your body needs a healthy amount of rest to recover and recoop. Allowing your brain and body to calm down and cool down is essential to helping you life a healthy life and leave you feeling great. Incorporating a mandatory sleep schedule into your daily routine will help to get your body on a healthy sleep cycle and allow you to get your z's in. Most studies recommend that the average adult should get at least seven to nine hours nightly in order to be fully rested and ready for the next day.
